Overview

Evangelical Lutheran Church Hong Kong (ELCHK) was faced with increasing demand for their home-care service due to the aging situation in Hong Kong. They needed to revamp service flow and offerings to increase workflow efficiency and service quality.I was responsible for the user research, design system development and UX/UI design for one of the three products, which increase internal workflow efficiency and facilitate collaboration among employees.

 

 

Background

In 2006, Jakob Nielsen pointed out that in most online communities, 90% of users are lurkers who never contribute, 9% of users contribute a little, and 1% of users account for almost all the action. In addition, there is also a problem of inequality in virtual communities, which means that the voices of the mostly silent are hard to hear.

Problem Finding

Virtual communities play an important role in academic discussion. At present, one of the great challenges faced by virtual communities is the availability of knowledge, which is mainly created through knowledge-sharing behavior.

 

 

Research Goals

  • Understand what platforms users generally use for knowledge-sharing behavior.
  • Measure the user experience of these knowledge-sharing platforms.
  • Analyze why users never or only contribute a little in the online communities.
  • Discover the pain points and needs in the process of knowledge-sharing behavior.

Questionaire

We first sent out a survey to understand what platforms users generally use for knowledge-sharing behavior. We got 42 responses, which helped us narrow down the topic. For example, users prefer to use Zhihu than academic forums because Zhihu has a better user experience..

Interview

We conducted interviews with 6 college students. Next, we conducted in-depth interviews with those users who were less motivated. We synthesized the collected data from the interviews into an affinity map to build personas, empathy maps, journey maps, and design ideas.
